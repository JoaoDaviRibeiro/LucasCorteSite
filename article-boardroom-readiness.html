Boardroom Readiness: Legal Checks Before Signing a Strategic Acquisition

A focused legal checklist for executives preparing to execute a strategic acquisition, from governance approvals to risk allocation and post-close integration.

Corporate May 10, 2026 7 min read

Executive Summary

Strategic acquisitions compress timelines and elevate risk. A disciplined legal workflow reduces exposure by aligning governance, diligence findings, and closing mechanics before signatures are exchanged.

Board-Level Approvals

Confirm corporate authority, verify fiduciary duties, and document approval mechanics. Align minutes, resolutions, and disclosure obligations with the transaction timeline.

  • Validate quorum and voting thresholds for approvals.
  • Confirm disclosure requirements and abstention rules.
  • Secure conflict-of-interest statements for directors.

Diligence and Risk Allocation

Map diligence findings to specific contract protections, including indemnities, escrow terms, and closing conditions. Prioritize high-impact liabilities and regulatory exposure.

  • Link diligence red flags to negotiated representations.
  • Define caps, baskets, and survival periods for claims.
  • Clarify regulatory approvals and long-stop dates.

Integration Safeguards

Ensure post-close integration clauses cover IP transfers, key employee retention, and operational continuity to avoid disruption after closing.
